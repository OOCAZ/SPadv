Slide Tell Back

 

Who:  One or two bilingual people, who know English (or French or Spanish or common language with the Accuracy Checker) will hold the phone and do the work for this phase.  It is best if these back translators were not involved in the translation of the story, the Story Tell Back, nor involved in the community checks. 

 

Purpose:  The purpose of the Slide Tell Back phase is to send audio or text retellings (back translations) in the source language for each slide to a Remote Oral Consultant Checker working via the ROCC web app.  Telling back the literal meaning of each slide phrase by phrase should help the Checker to discern how accurately and meaningfully each slide was translated into the local language.  The Slide Tell Back audio files will compliment the Story Tell Back (back translation) and give the Accuracy Checker a more detailed version of each slide translation draft to compare to the source text.

 

Activities: 

  1. The back translator(s) should play each slide’s translation in the local language, perhaps pausing after each phrase to tap the mic button at the bottom of the screen to give a literal translation of the meaning in the Gateway language. 
  2. Pause the slide tell back recording by tapping the two vertical lines pause button.
  3. Resume the phrase-by-phrase slide tell back recording by tapping the mic+ button.
  4. Stop or finish the recording by tapping the square button. 
  5. If you need to start over with the slide back translation, tap the square stop button to refresh the mic button.  If the mic button has no + next to it, it is ready to restart the recording.  If the mic button has a + next to it, it is ready to resume the audio recording in progress.
  6. For each slide, tap the up-arrow-in-the-cloud icon to send the Slide Tell Back audio recording to the Accuracy Checker / ROCC.  When the phone has connectivity or is online, the file will be sent. A tick mark will replace the arrow in the cloud and the cloud will turn blue when ROCC has received the audio file. ??
  7. To write a text back translation for each slide, tap on the line just under the picture and playback button to bring up the keyboard.
  8. To send a text back translation, tap the white paper airplane / right pointing arrow icon to the right of the texting area.  Does the airplane icon turn color when the text has been sent?  Textual back translations will show up as texts in purple color in the Remote Check phase.
  9. Swipe down to the Remote Check  phase or use the drop down menu on the top of the screen to tap to the Remote Check phase.
  10. A warning will display if the user enters the Remote Check phase before all the slide back translations have been sent.

 

Notes:

●     Tapping the mic while the local language translation is playing will automatically pause the local translation playback.  Also, tapping the play button to resume listening to the draft translation will automatically stop the slide tell back recording.  The app will not allow both playback and audio recording to take place at the same time. 

●     If the back translator(s) know that the Accuracy Checker (ROCC) would prefer a text back translation, they can type out and send a written back translation instead of doing the audio recorded back translation.  The audio/oral back translation is easier for the language team, but having a written text back translation is easier for the Accuracy Checker.

●     Text back translations are much less costly to send to the ROCC than audio/oral back translations.  Text files are much smaller than audio files.

●     When the ROCC has approved a slide, the gray tick mark will turn green for that slide.  The ROCCer controls all slide approvals.  Slide approvals can be reversed by the ROCCer and the tick mark will revert back to gray.  When all the slides are approved, the Voice Studio and other final production phases will be unlocked.
